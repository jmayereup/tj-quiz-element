Pay attention to the different types of volcanoes. ---text Volcanoes A volcano is like a mountain that opens downward to a pool of molten rock below the surface of the Earth. When pressure builds up, eruptions occur. Gases and rock shoot up through the opening and spill over or fill the air with lava fragments. Hot liquid rock under the Earth's surface is called magma. When it comes out of the volcano, it is called lava. Volcanoes can be active, dormant, or extinct. An active volcano can erupt at any time. A dormant volcano has not erupted recently but might again. An extinct volcano will not erupt again. Many volcanoes are in the Ring of Fire, which is an area around the edge of the Pacific Ocean. ---questions-4 Q: What causes an eruption? Matter usually comes in three different forms, or states. These states are solid, liquid, and gas. A solid has a shape that does not change easily. A rock, a book, and a pencil are examples of solids. A liquid does not have its own shape. It takes the shape of its container. Milk and juice are liquids. A gas moves freely and spreads out to fill any space. The air we breathe is a gas. Some substances, like water, can be a solid, a liquid, or a gas at different temperatures. ---vocab matter: the physical stuff that everything is made of state: a condition or form of something (like solid or liquid) container: an object like a box or bottle that holds things substance: a particular kind of matter or material temperature: how hot or cold something is ---cloze-4 1. A *solid* keeps its shape and does not change easily. 2. A *liquid* takes the shape of its container. 3. A *gas* moves freely to fill any space. 4. Have you ever wondered what things are made of? Everything in the universe is made of tiny particles called atoms. Atoms are very, very small. A single human hair is much wider than an atom. In fact, one hair is as thick as 500,000 atoms! An atom has a center called the core. Inside the core, there are protons and neutrons. There are also electrons. The electrons move around the core very fast. Here is a surprise: Atoms are mostly empty space. If we remove the empty space, all the people in the world could fit inside a teaspoon. It is hard to imagine, but it is true! ---vocab particle: a very tiny piece of something core: the center part of an object electron: a tiny part of an atom that moves fast universe: all of space and everything in it imagine: to make a picture of something in your mind ---cloze-4 1.
